# Privacy Policy — Slate

**Last updated:** April 2026
**Contact:** belkis.aslani@gmail.com

---

## Your data stays on your device

Slate runs AI models entirely on your phone. Your conversations, prompts, and responses are never sent to any server. There is no cloud processing.

## What Slate stores locally

Slate stores the following data on your device only:

- **Downloaded AI model files** — stored in app-private storage
- **Chat conversations** — if chat history is enabled in Settings
- **App preferences** — theme, settings choices

All data is stored in app-private directories that other apps cannot access. When you uninstall Slate, all data is automatically deleted by the operating system.

## Network access

Slate connects to the internet **only** to download AI model files from Hugging Face (huggingface.co). These downloads use HTTPS encryption. No other network requests are made.

Slate does not:
- Send your conversations anywhere
- Contact analytics services
- Transmit device identifiers
- Make background network requests

## No tracking

Slate contains:
- No analytics SDKs
- No advertising identifiers
- No crash reporting services
- No third-party tracking code

We do not collect, transmit, or sell any user data.

## No accounts

Slate does not require an account, login, or registration. There is no user identifier associated with your usage.

## Your controls

You have full control over your data:

- **Disable chat history** in Settings to prevent conversation storage
- **Clear all chat history** from Settings at any time
- **Delete downloaded models** from the Storage screen
- **Uninstall the app** to remove all data completely

## No telemetry

Slate ships with zero telemetry. There is no opt-in or opt-out because there is nothing to opt into. If telemetry is ever introduced in a future version, it will require explicit user consent and will be off by default.

## Open source

Slate's source code is available for inspection. You can verify that the app behaves exactly as described in this policy.
